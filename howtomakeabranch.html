    How To Make a Branch in Webstorm


    In this section I will show you how to make a branch in webstorm to eventually be uploaded to github. First I should define what a branch is:


    Branch: A branch is a parallel version of a repository. It is contained within the repository, but does not affect the primary or master branch allowing you to work freely without disrupting the "live" version. When you've made the changes you want to make, you can merge your branch back into the master branch to publish your changes.


    Master Branch: Your master branch is your main branch where all your code goes that you know works. Every other branch will be eventually merged into the master branch. is this working


    When you start your project you will automatically be in your master Branch which you can see on the bottom right of webstorm. To create a new branch you would click where it says master on the bottom right, then click, "+ New Branch". You will then be prompted with a box to name your new branch. I named mine "explainHowToMakeNewBranch".


    You will now see your new branch. You can now make changed freely without worrying about messing up your master file. Once you like what you've done and want to commit to github, you can follow the instructions on the how to make a commit page of this tutorial. You can then also merge this branch into the master which I will cover in another section.
